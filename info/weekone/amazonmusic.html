Amazon Music: Customer Retention

Overview

How might we gamify Amazon Music so that we can improve user retention and provide a unique music streaming experience?

We aim to...Tune In.

Duration: 4 weeks

Team Members: Shasteny Maclang and Federico Sarno

My Contributions:

Design Considerations

1. Efficient Tracking Method

A more labour-saving tracking method for logging cycle information. This creates more accessibility and time efficiency.

2. Privacy and Security

Mindfulness towards the lack of current laws protecting those with a uterus, and maintain full transparency with users about what happens to their data.

3. Health and Medical Accuracy

Prioritizing reliable educational content that informs users about their cycle, symptoms and fertility predictions accurately.

Feature Developments

  • A more detailed calendar view, offering users an at-a-glance summary of their cycle, symptoms, and ovulation predictions.
  • A dedicated Fertility Tracking page, with tools that log ovulation, pregnancy, fertility symptoms, and any related habits such as IVF tracking or temperature charting.
  • A new Resources section, that gives users access to medically-sound articles, tips related to fertility health and a section that informs users of how their data is being used.

Reflections

What Worked Well

  • Implementing Summary and Log tabs created a clear and easy-to-read layout.
  • Could visually see users’ clicking patterns which allowed us to receive valuable feedback to further adjust our redesign.

What Did Not Work Well

  • Our observations did not match our design frame needs. Being within a medical space limited our ability to gauge behaviors since we could not enter past waiting room(s).
  • Users did not understand the subsections of the Health Resources Page. This seemed to be due to the vocabulary and how the options were named within our prototype.
