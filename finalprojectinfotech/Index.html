Welcome!

Welcome to our musical website! Our website is dedicated to teaching and informing the public more about music from different ages in history and diverse languages! Feel free to discover our other pages that explore Russian, Spanish and Italian music! You'll get to learn about their history, their most popular genres, common instruments used in many of their songs, many interesting fun facts about the evolution of each musical language, top artists, new releases and many extra diverse resources! So come on in and let's get to listening to some funky jams! Remember to also check out the timeline below and don't forget to also generate some random songs below to further explore music from around the world!

About the Authors

Lauren Holmes

Lauren Holmes is from San Diego, California. She is currently a graduate student in Library and Information Sciences at Pratt Institute in New York City and completed her Bachelor's in English at San Diego State University and her Master's in Interdisciplinary Studies, Experimental Humanities and Social Engagement, at New York University. Lauren contributed to the Russian language page and parts of the homepage. Lauren's interest in Russian rock music history stems from an academic interest in historiography regarding the Eastern Bloc, the inner-workings of state socialism, and a deep love for rock music as resistance and escapism from oppressive regimes. She is currently learning Russian.

Marilyn Ray

MJ is a current graduate student in the Museums and Digital Culture program in the School of Information. She received her bachelors in Anthropology from Texas A&M University in 2024. While in undergrad, she worked in the Collections Department at the George Bush Presidential Library and Museum. In her personal life, she enjoys crocheting, playing Stardew Valley, and hanging out with her cat, Pedro. Her special interests include museum collection management, digital preservation, and metadata upkeep. After graduation, she hopes to work in a registrar or collections management position in a history museum or archive. She MJ contributed to the Spanish language page and parts of the home page. Her fascination with Spanish language music stems from listening to music in Spanish with her mother, who was fluent in the language after spending 5 years in Honduras. Spanish music is full of passion and encompasses many different cultures and that is what she loves most about it.

Jasmin Rose Guerrera

Jasmin Rose Guerrera is from Montreal Quebec, Canada. She is a student at Pratt Institute currently pursuing her graduate in Information Experience Design in New York City and completed her undergraduate degree in Film Animation at Concordia University back in Montreal. Considering Jasmin comes from a family of Italian descent and speaks the language, she contributed to the making and development of the Italian language page. She also worked on the inner workings of the timeline and parts of the home page. Lastly, there were also some contributions made to the Italian page from Jasmin’s 92 year-old Nonna, Rosa Boiardi, who was excited to share some of her favourite Italian artists with the world as well.
