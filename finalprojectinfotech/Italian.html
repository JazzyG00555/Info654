Brief History

Italy is known for having a strong cultural and global influence full of tradition, delicious food, exquisite dance moves and of course, amazing music! Even though the country was just starting to form, troubadours travelled throughout diverse parts of Europe spreading their music. This led to the creation of Italian folk music which began its debut by grasping the public attention with its notable popular subgenres like, the Tarantella. During the 16th century and 17th century, opera also began taking the stage with famous opera houses, such as La Scala opening up for the very first time. As time went on, Italo Disco then made an appearance in the 1970s as Italy started branching out and becoming influenced by the western world. This eventually led to newer artists involved in pop and rock music in the 21st century such as Il Volo and Måneskin.

Interesting Facts

  • The "Tarantella", one of the most known genres of Italian music from Southern Italy, translates to "Spider" or "Tarantula". The "Tarantella" is also a traditional dance with the goal of curing women who have been bitten by a tarantula.
  • Italy has won Eurovision, a musical contest, three times. The musicians who have won include Gigliola Cinquetti with Non ho l'étà (1964), Toto Cutugno with 1992 (1990) and Måneskin with Zitti e Buoni (2021).
  • Italy hosts multiple music festivals including Arena di Verona Festival, Umbria Jazz festival, and The Festival dei Due Mondi.
  • A German label known as, ZYX, was the one to pinpoint and give Italo Disco its name.
  • Antonio Vivaldi, a violinist as well as a composer, was nicknamed the "Red Priest".
