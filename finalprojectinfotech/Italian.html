A Brief History

by Jasmin Rose Guerrera

Italy is known for having a strong cultural and global influence full of tradition, delicious food, exquisite dance moves and of course, amazing music! When the country was just beginning to form, Italian music began its development and storytelling through troubadours. Troubadours were musical poets who travelled throughout diverse parts of Europe. They spread their poems across the nation leading to the creation of Italian folk music which began its debut by grasping public attention with its notable popular subgenres like, the Tarantella. During the 16th century and 17th century, opera also began taking the stage with famous opera houses, such as Teatro Alla Scala opening up in Milan for the very first time. This led to new composers such as Antonio Vivaldi , who further shaped Italian opera with compositions such as “Orlando Finto Pazzo” and “Ottone in Villa”. As time went on, Italo Disco was the next influential genre to make an appearance in the 1970s to 1980s. This was due to the fact that Italy was just beginning to branch out and become more influenced by the western world. This eventually led to newer artists involved in pop and rock music in the 21st century such as Il Volo and Måneskin.

Interesting Facts

  • The "Tarantella" , one of the most known genres of Italian music from Southern Italy, translates to "Spider" or "Tarantula". The "Tarantella" is also a traditional dance with the goal of curing women who have been bitten by a tarantula.
  • Italy has won Eurovision, a musical contest, three times. The musicians who have won include Gigliola Cinquetti with "Non ho l'étà" (1964), Toto Cutugno with "1992" (1990) and Måneskin with "Zitti e Buoni" (2021).
  • Italy hosts multiple music festivals including Arena di Verona Festival, Umbria Jazz festival, and The Festival dei Due Mondi.
  • A German label known as, ZYX, was the one to pinpoint and give Italo Disco its name.
  • Antonio Vivaldi, a violinist as well as a composer for opera and classical music, was nicknamed the "Red Priest".
