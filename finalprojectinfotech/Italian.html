Brief History

Italy is known for having a strong cultural and global influence full of delicious food, exquisite dances and of course, amazing music! Even though the country was just starting to form, troubadours travelled throughout diverse parts of Europe spreading their music. This led to the creation of Italian folk music with its notable popular subgenres like, the Tarantella. During the 16th century and 17th century, opera also began taking the stage with famous opera houses, such as La Scala opening up for the very first time. As time went on, Italy started branching out and becoming influenced by the western world. This eventually led to newer artists involved in pop and rock music in the 21st century.

Interesting Facts

  • The "Tarantella", one of the most known genres of Italian music from Southern Italy, translates to "Spider" or "Tarantula". The "Tarantella" is also a traditional dance with the goal of curing women who have been bitten by a tarantula.
  • Antonio Vivaldi, a violinist as well as a composer, was nicknamed the "Red Priest".
